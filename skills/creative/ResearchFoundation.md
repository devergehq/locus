# Creative — Research Foundation

Creative work benefits from structured research, not from abstract inspiration. This document lists the foundations worth consulting when the task warrants deeper grounding.

## On constraint and creativity

- **Stokes, *Creativity From Constraints* (2005)** — systematic survey of how imposed constraint produces original work across domains (art, science, engineering).
- **Sweller, Cognitive Load Theory** — constraint reduces working memory burden, permitting deeper exploration within the reduced space.
- **TRIZ (Altshuller)** — 40 inventive principles derived from patent analysis; many are constraint-transformation tools.

## On taste vs craft

- **The Cult of Done manifesto** — emphasises shipping. Taste is tested by finishing.
- **Paul Graham, "Taste for Makers" (2002)** — taxonomy of good taste as a set of characterisations (elegance, simplicity, hard-but-not-arbitrary).
- **Rick Rubin, *The Creative Act* (2023)** — practical framing of taste as attention, not judgement.

## On iteration discipline

- **Lean UX / Lean Startup** (Reis, Gothelf) — build-measure-learn as applied to product, equally applicable to creative output.
- **Pixar's "Braintrust"** (Ed Catmull, *Creativity, Inc.*) — structured peer critique in service of iteration; separates proposal from identity.

## On reference, inspiration, and influence

- **Cognitive Flexibility Theory (Spiro et al. 1992)** — expertise is enabled by flexible re-representation of concepts across contexts; reference is re-representation fuel.
- **Hofstadter, *Gödel, Escher, Bach*** — the role of cross-domain analogical thinking in original thought.
- **Lakoff, *Metaphors We Live By*** — how the metaphors available to a thinker shape what can be thought.

## On originality and the adjacent possible

- **Stuart Kauffman, adjacent possible** — innovation emerges from what is one step removed from existing knowledge, not from nothing. Applies to creative work as much as biology.
- **Steven Johnson, *Where Good Ideas Come From*** — long hunches, networked ideas, and the slow accretion of the adjacent possible.

## On AI-era specifics

- The persona-prompting evidence summarised in the Research cycle for this project: named personalities do not help reasoning; functional traits do. This applies to creative agents too — a "creative" persona with backstory adds drift without benefit; traits like `iterative + exploratory + analogical` shape output measurably.
- Over-reliance on AI generation produces regression to a culturally-averaged output. The specificity and constraint principles in `Principles.md` are countermeasures.

## On knowing when to stop

- Satisficing (Simon, 1956) — the art of recognising "good enough" as distinct from optimum.
- The 15-minute rule (debugging, but generalisable) — after a time budget, if progress has stalled, the problem is often structural, not craft.

---

This document is a starting map, not a syllabus. When a creative task warrants deeper reading, pick one or two entries above and actually read them — surface-level quoting of references is the opposite of the discipline this skill is for.
